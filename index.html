Letter To MAHI

Dear Mahi,
I hope this letter finds you in the midst of a quiet moment with your favorite satire.

It's my way of saying I see you, and I admire the spark you bring to everything. First, I know relationships aren't on your radar right now, and I respect that completely. Life's phases are temporary, and I'm not here to rush.

That said, I do like you—a lot. Not in a way that demands anything, but in the quiet way that makes me want to know more about the real you. The stories behind your Lucknow-Delhi roots, the chaos of your study breaks, your preference, your likes and dislikes, or even the simple things like what makes a perfect plate of momos for you (steamed, fried, or that spicy tandoori twist?).

If our friendship had a constitution, I would keep it simple

Article 1- No pressure
Article 2- Extra teekhi chutney allowed
Article 3- Feelings can be expressed, never imposed
Article 4- Freedom first, connection second, it's ur choice

No deadlines, no expectations, just pure intention

I’m not here to complicate your life. If friendship is all this ever becomes, I’d still consider that lucky.

If this little letter makes you smile even a tiny bit, or if you ever feel like sharing a momo recommendation, a random thought, or just venting—I'm right here, no schemes, just sincere.

Whether this becomes a conversation, a friendship, or just a quiet page on the internet , I’m glad I wrote it.

Nexda (or whatever silly name you want to call me )
